Team setup guide

Truck Simulatorrunning in a snap.

Turn your Mac or PC into a virtual Matrack tracker — the ELD app connects to it over Bluetooth, just like the real device. No hardware. Pick your computer below and follow four steps.

⚠️
Test account & test server only. Never sign in with a real driver's account or production data, and never trigger a DOT / FMCSA data transfer.
01

Set it up

1

Download & unzip

Grab the app and double-click the zip to unzip it → MatrackTruckSim.app.

⬇ Download for Mac · 0.9 MB · universal
2

Open it (first time only)

Right-click the app → Open → Open. macOS asks once because it's an in-house app — this is safe.

If it's still blocked: System Settings → Privacy & Security → “Open Anyway.”
When asked, allow Bluetooth.
3

Name your Mac ELD-MA

System Settings → General → About → Name → set it to e.g. ELD-MA-You. Then toggle Bluetooth off and on.

The app finds the tracker by this name — it must start with ELD-MA.
4

Connect your phone

Open the app — it shows “Advertising as ELD-MA.” On the phone (test account): ELD app → Bluetooth → pick ELD-MA → connect. 🎉

02

What you can do

🚚

Drive

Engine on, set a speed, or auto-cruise a route on the live map.

🎬

Scenarios

21 canned situations — idle, power cycles, GPS loss, stored backlog — one click each.

📡

Signal & Drop

Weaken the signal, or DROP to force a real out-of-range disconnect, then reconnect.

📦

Stored Dump

Replay stored packets fast (≈500 ms) to reproduce the disconnect bug; 1 s is safe.

03

Stuck?

macOS won't open the app
It's an in-house app (unsigned), so macOS warns once. Right-click → Open → Open, or System Settings → Privacy & Security → Open Anyway. It's safe.
My phone doesn't see “ELD-MA”
Make sure the computer is named ELD-MA… and Bluetooth is on. Toggle the computer's Bluetooth off/on. On iPhone, you can also Settings → Bluetooth → Forget the old device, then reconnect.
Only one phone connects at a time
One computer = one truck. For several people driving at the same time, each driver needs their own computer running the app (each named ELD-MA…).
The connection drops on its own
That's expected if you pressed DROP (out-of-range) or ran a stored-dump at ≈500 ms. Press BACK to reconnect, or use 1 s cadence to avoid the disconnect.
